Issue Attitudes

Abortion

The survey also asks about the legal consequences for abortion providers. Within the Democratic and Independent categories, approximately 3/4 oppose or are not sure. On the other hand, a majority -- 60% -- of Republicans support these consequences.


Attitudes towards an abortion ban, across partisan stripes, are varied. While Republicans are more supportive of a complete ban, the percent reporting this is 32%. The most extreme response is endorsed by 32% of sampled Republicans. Similarly, 37% of sample Democrats support no limitations or restrictions. Majorities in both parties favor something less extreme such as restrictions after the first and second trimesters.

Affective Polarization

Respondents completed a "feeling thermometer." This measure ranges from 1 to 10. The image below shows the means for several partisan groups. Hover over the bars to display mean estimates.

Those at the partisan extremes tend to be hostile towards elites, and to a lesser extent, "ordinary" members of the competing party. However, affective polarization -- liking the inparty and loathing the outparty -- is by no means restricted to politicians. It seems to apply to all outparty members.
