1.2.2 Issue Attitudes

Abortion

This question asks about the legal consequences for abortion providers. Within the Democratic and Independent categories, approximately 3/4 oppose or are not sure. On the other hand, a majority -- 60% -- of Republicans support these consequences.


Attitudes towards an abortion ban, across partisan stripes, are varied. While Republicans are more supportive of a complete ban, the percent reporting this is 32%. The most extreme response is endorsed by 32% of sampled Republicans. Similarly, 37% of sample Democrats support no limitations or restrictions. Majorities in both parties favor something less extreme such as restrictions after the first and second trimesters.

Guns

There is widespread bipartisan support for raising the minimum age to own an assault rifle.

There is not bipartisan support for restricting ownership.

1.3 Findings

  • Donald Trump's endorsement of Kari Lake and Black Masters resonated with his supporters.
  • Clear partisan differences emerge on gun regulation and abortion policies.
  • Extreme partisans -- on both sides -- are generally quite hostile towards elites and members of the opposing party.
  • Important differences emerge in the perceived ideological orientation of the parties.

2. Party Registration in Arizona

\ link to this section

The 2022 is the first federal election to follow the approved congressional districts [this wording needs to be checked.] The figures below show the partisan composition of the new congressional districts. Each slice represents a district. Hover over a slice to display information about that district. The size of each slice is scaled by a measure I call partisan tilt. This is a pretty silly name, but it doesn't really matter, so long as the measure is understood. It's the ratio of registered Republicans to registered Democrats (measured following the 2020 election). These numbers need to be regularly updated -- and that's an ongoing project -- but for now, this will do. A larger slice represents a district that that has proportionately more registered Republicans than Democrats. Likewise, a tiny slice represents a district in which registered Democrats outnumber registered Republicans. A score of 1 represents a district that is balanced. There are as many Democrats as Republicans. What about Independents? After all, they generally outnumber members of a party. If one hovers over a slice, there is a measure called the swing score. I know. It's nonsense. Nonetheless, the swing score is the ratio of Independents to partisans in a district. If that's confusing, each slice shows the proportion of Republicans, Democrats, and Independents.
